Design Process

Our group went through a few design cycles as we developed our idea for a virtual bartering experience. Initially, we tried to design for all types of users, trying to make it easier for anyone to barter. From our competitive analysis and initial user research, we determined breakdowns and areas left uncharted. To address these breadowns and needs, we came up with a list of functionality and requirements we wished our site to have.

We then performed brick and mortar observations and spoke to a few expert traders. From this research, we narrowed down our user base to only collectors as they trade often anyway and normally go to great lengths to complete their collection. This additional research produced a new list of breakdowns that we wanted to mend and provided inspiration for our personas and scenarios.

In the next phase of our design process, we developed wireframes detailing the specific interactions for our site. We used our personas to guide many of our design decisions and to solve any of internal arguments about which features were essential to our users.
