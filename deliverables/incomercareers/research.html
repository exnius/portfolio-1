Q: When you were looking for jobs were you looking both in the US and elsewhere?

Marcus, US Native Physician

Q: Tell me about the credentialing process for a foreign physician wanting to work in the US?

A: To practice medicine in the United States, you need three things at a minimum:
• Medical License (grants the right to practice medicine, diagnose illness, and prescribe treatment)
• DEA Registration (grants prescribing privileges for restricted meds, e.g. narcotics)
• Malpractice Insurance

It's important to note that of those three things, only the DEA registration is federal. Malpractice and Licensure are state-specific. So my license to practice medicine (and the insurance which the law requires me to carry to lawfully practice medicine) are adjudicated by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.

So forget Canada. If I want to practice medicine in West Virginia and Ohio, I need to get licensed (and get insurance coverage) in both of those states in addition to Pennsylvania. The name for this process is "credentialing". Usage: "I can't start at the Morgantown clinic until I get credentialed in West Virginia, which will take another six weeks." Note that credentialling is not just about the magic pieces of paper. It also usually includes (at a minimum) a criminal background check, a loss run history (insurance talk for a check to see if any insurance company has ever had to pay a malpractice claim on you) and other particulars (e.g. in Pennsylvania, you have to be fingerprinted and go into a special database if you're working with children).

The Career Center rarely encounters istudents who have trouble finding employers that hire internationals. Typically, employers prioritize skill set; if you’re talented, we’ll take you and worry about documentation later.

in your TT profile, you indicate citizenship--this is also checked by university records. If you are not a U.S. citizen, job listings that require citizenship are listed, but you are not given the option to apply (Apply button is not there). This can be a helpful filter for international students to save time in the job hunt.
