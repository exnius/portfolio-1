My Process

When systems get complex and unwieldy, the user experience (UX) can become poor if left unguided. Poor UX can lead to sadness and frustration. When it comes to designing better experiences, process is everything. For certain strategies like contextual research and design, I work best in teams. When done right, collaboration spawns the most innovative ideas. It's like working with your fellow playmates to build sandcastles. Except now, it's based on better foundation: user research.

Research
Using HCI methods like contextual inquiries, retrospective interviews, and cultural probes, we can explore exactly how people work. Many of these ethnography techniques have been used in human factors to better understand the complexity of tasks like flying planes. We can apply these techniques to understand existing workflows so that we can design technologies to support and extend them.
Interpret
After gathering the data, we need to make sense of it in the context of the project. We can do this several ways depending on the type of data. Typically, affinity diagrams help the team quickly draw big ideas from a large set of qualitative data. Drawing models can help us understand taskflow, cultural elements, and role organization. Once the big ideas emerge, we can start to design brand new ideas or fix broken ones.
Build
My favorite part. It's always exciting to see your ideas come to life. Interpretation of the data helps to inform and inspire better ideas. Before fully implementing, it's a good idea to create rough prototypes. This means sketching, wireframing, and paper prototyping before getting into higher levels of fidelity. These help to communicate ideas to each other and to quickly iterate on them. The hard part is knowing when to stop. That's when experience comes to play.
Test
Evaluating a design may be the most neglected part of the process. It's important too! Evaluation techniques should especially be used during prototyping to make sure they don't carry problems into next iterations. We can apply methods like think-aloud, heuristic evaluations, and well-designed surveys to test a final design. Major problems are noted and fixed before delivery. Ideally, this phase cycles back to Interpret for effective iterative design.

Selected Works

Thinkspace

iPad app for biology students

Role: Lead User Experience Design
Design Problem: Using SRI International's AI reasoning technology and knowledge base, develop an engaging application for AP Biology students through a user-centered process.

In a collaborative effort with SRI International, our interdisciplinary team of four Master students from Carnegie Mellon University's Human Computer Interaction Institute set out to understand the learning dynamics that occur between teachers and students in biology classrooms and how SRI's artificial intelligence could be best applied.

Roomy

iPhone App for Parents in Transition

Role: Interaction Designer
Design Problem: Design a solution to help parents declutter their homes after their children move out.

Upon interpreting data from contextual inquiries, think-alouds, and key-stroke level models, our team revealed specific needs for home archiving. Our solution involves a mobile application for parents to easily find out how to sort their children's belongings.

Age of Waste

A Visualization of My Waste Habits Over Two Weeks

Exercise: Graphic Design
Design Problem: Create an infographic that depicts your waste habits over a two week period.

Over the span of two weeks, I kept track of the items I threw away. I extrapolated from this data to see how much trash would be left on Earth after my death (morbidly estimated age). Seeing that some types of materials take longer to decompose than others, it turned out to be an interesting visualization and a useful reminder about my footprint.

Storyphone

Designing an Interactive Voice Response System (IVRS) to Preserve Storytelling

Role: Interaction Designer
Design Problem: Design an object, interface, system, or service intended to help us appreciate our differences (CHI2011 Student Design Competition).

For CHI's Student Design Competition 2011, a group of us at CMU teamed up with students at DAIICT to submit Storyphone, an interactive voice response system (IVRS) built to capture oral stories from around the world. Storyphone users can call in to either contribute their stories to the archive or listen to stories submitted by others. Our proposed system is a novel use of mobile technology to help us appreciate differences through oral storytelling.

Livescribe PulsePen

Designing for Novices of New Digital Pen Technology

Role: Interaction Designer
Design Problem: Employ a user-centered design process to evaluate and redesign the experience of using Livescribe's Digital PulsePen.

A group of us at UCSD had the opportunity to employ rapid contextual design techniques to evaluate and redesign the Pulse pen experience. In the process, we discovered a number of non-intuitive elements in the experience and envisioned new designs to solve these problems. As one of my very first exposures to user centered design, this project served to inspire and lead me towards a path in HCI.

Summarization Tool

Optimizing Web Search for Academic Papers

Role: Interaction Designer
Design Problem: Employ a user-centered design process to evaluate and redesign a web application that helps scholars perform literature reviews.

This tool from the Language Technologies Institute aims to facilitate scholars in conducting literature reviews by providing intelligent summaries relevant to a user's research. As part of a team of MHCI students, I used contextual design techniques to explore the needs of such users, evaluate the existing interface, and prototype for future iterations. To understand user's workflow better, we interviewed PhD students who had experience or were in the process of conducting literature reviews.

Barter Boutique

A Web Application for Collectors to Barter Items and Collections

Role: Interaction Designer
Design Problem: Explore the boutique retail space and design a transactional web site that will benefit from some aspect of personalization.

For a project in Interaction Design, we had the opportunity to design a website in the domain of "boutique retail." Instead of taking this in the traditional direction, our research helped us discover more interesting needs in trading. Our final idea, Barter Boutique, is a website that connects a community of collectors and enables them to trade items within their collection with each other.

TritonLink

Redesign of UCSD's Student Portal Webpage

Role: User Experience Design Intern
Design Problem: Redesign UCSD's student portal webpage based on results from focus groups and surveys.

As an intern at UC San Diego's web office, I had the opportunity to redesign the student portal site. After looking at survey responses, analytics, feedback from focus groups and conducting a heuristic evaluation, I generated three rounds of wireframes that addressed the data.

Sketching

Pieces from a Course on Sketching

Design, especially in the world of HCI, involves the ability to communicate ideas clearly. To develop my skills in visually communicating ideas, I took a four month course at Carnegie Mellon called Methodology of Visualization. The course helped me develop abilities in deconstructing visual artifacts, perspective drawing, basic rendering, visual narratives, and storyboarding.

HiFive

Mobile Concept for Conference Attendees

Role: Interaction Designer
Design Problem: Discover mobile opportunities for connecting people in the same space.

As part of a course jointly taught by John Zimmerman and Jim Morris, our interdiscplinary team of five spent four months researching an idea for a mobile application. The course was an excellent exercise in taking an idea from infancy, iterating on it based on research, and pitching to an audience. In the process, we discovered a real unmet need in the mobile world, defined the technical feasibility, and determined the financial viability of our idea.

Incomer Careers

Mobile Concept for International Job Seekers

Role: Interaction Designer
Design Problem: Design a mobile application to help internationals find employment in the United States.

For a project in Interaction Design, a group of us set out to solve problems for foreigners residing in the U.S. Our assignment involved problems in the domain of employment. We went through several iterations for the scenarios we created in response to interviews, market research, critique feedback.

Simple Garnishes, Simple Recipes

A Recipe Book for Simple Garnishes

Exercise: Graphic Design
Design Problem: Create a themed recipe book to demonstrate proficiency in color theory, layout, and typography.

As the final project for a communication design class, I created a recipebook demonstrating principles of color theory, grid layout, and typography. The assignment called for a cover page, introduction page, table of contents, and ten recipes all revolving around the same theme. I chose "simple garnishes, simple recipes."
